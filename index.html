Shared Field Intelligence for Public Safety Teams

A² Perceptions is developing a wearable XR mission-awareness platform that helps teams navigate the search area, capture witness information, correlate live feeds, and coordinate around a shared operational picture.

Why it matters

Field teams still piece the mission together across disconnected tools.

Search and rescue operations often depend on radios, interviews, maps, drones, trail cameras, canine teams, and handwritten notes. A² is exploring how the most important mission context can be surfaced in one wearable workflow instead of spread across separate devices and channels.

01

Navigation friction

Responders need route cues, clue locations, and last-seen areas without constantly looking down at a separate map.

02

Interview drift

Witness details can be incomplete, inconsistent, or hard to compare once multiple teams are collecting information.

03

Feed overload

Drone video, trail cams, canine views, and teammate feeds are powerful, but hard to fuse into one clear alert.

04

Planning gaps

Teams need a common picture that links terrain, tasks, status, and extraction plans as the search evolves.

Concept workflows

Four core workflows currently shape the platform direction.

These concept UIs point to a clearer product story: navigate, capture, correlate, and coordinate. Together they better reflect the workflows A² is aiming to support in the field.

01 · Navigate

Navigation

Guide responders with search sectors, waypoints, team positions, clue markers, and extraction routing directly in view.

02 · Capture

Interview Assist

Capture witness statements, summarize key facts, and compare interview details across teams with LLM-assisted cross-reference.

03 · Correlate

Multi-Feed Assist

Pull cues from team feeds, drones, trail cams, and K9 cameras so the responder can see alerts and broadcast high-priority findings.

04 · Coordinate

Collaborative Map

Bring teams together around a shared 3D terrain map for search planning, route synchronization, and common mission understanding.

See

Surface live mission context like search sectors, last-seen points, and route cues in the responder’s line of sight.

Capture

Record witness details, voice notes, and field observations so they can become structured mission inputs.

Correlate

Use AI assistance to compare feeds, summarize interviews, score confidence, and flag important cues.

Coordinate

Share plan updates, team status, and alerts across responders and field leadership from a common operational picture.

What we are building

A shared operating picture delivered at the edge.

The concept is not just a heads-up display. It is a mission loop that links wearable XR, AI-assisted interpretation, team-level awareness, and collaborative map planning. The result should be faster sensemaking in the field and clearer coordination across the team.

  • Compass, search sector, waypoint, and extraction guidance.
  • Interview capture with structured facts like last seen, clothing, and direction of travel.
  • Cross-feed correlation across drone, trail cam, canine, and teammate video.
  • 3D collaborative terrain views for route planning and synchronized execution.
