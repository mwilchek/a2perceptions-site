Platform concept

Wearable XR for shared mission awareness.

The updated A² platform concept is increasingly centered on four connected SAR capabilities: navigation in the field, interview intelligence, multi-feed awareness, and collaborative map planning.

View ModulesWhy It Matters

Operational modules

A clearer product story is emerging from the concept set.

Rather than a single generic XR overlay, the platform can be understood as a family of connected mission experiences that each support a critical part of the search workflow.

Module 01

Navigation

Compass, bearings, waypoints, search sectors, team positions, clue markers, extraction cues, and local mission status.

Module 02

Interview Assist

Transcript capture, key-fact extraction, LLM summarization, cross-reference between interviews, and map-linked witness context.

Module 03

Multi-Feed Assist

Live teammate, drone, trail-cam, and K9 feeds with AI overlays, confidence scoring, and alert broadcast controls.

Module 04

Collaborative Map

Shared 3D terrain planning with route summaries, team status, confidence rollups, and synchronized tasking.

System layers

Designed as an integrated mission system, not only a headset app.

These concepts imply a platform stack that spans the XR wearer, supporting compute, AI services, external feeds, and a team-level coordination layer. The value comes from how those parts connect, not from any one screen alone.

01

Wearable XR client

The responder-facing view surfaces glanceable mission context, team state, and interaction points while keeping attention on the field.

02

Mission data layer

Locations, zones, clues, witness facts, last-seen points, search status, and task assignments become structured shared data.

03

AI assistance layer

Summarization, cross-reference, detection overlays, confidence scores, and alert prioritization help reduce information overload.

04

External sensor inputs

Drone video, trail cameras, canine-mounted cameras, teammate feeds, and future modalities expand what the team can perceive.

Capability model

Sense. Structure. Correlate. Coordinate. Review.

Sense

Ingest location, bearings, mission status, team positions, witness conversation, and live sensor feeds from the field.

Structure

Turn raw inputs into usable mission elements such as key facts, clue markers, last-seen points, route plans, and team tasks.

Correlate

Use AI support to compare interviews, detections, and feed cues so the most relevant signals rise above noise.

Coordinate

Synchronize alerts, maps, team status, and route changes across responders and field leadership from a common picture.

Review

Preserve mission events, route history, confidence markers, and observations for debrief, training, and platform refinement.

Information already visible in the concepts

The target data model is becoming more specific.

Across the four UI concepts, the same kinds of mission information appear repeatedly. That is a useful signal for platform design because it suggests a stable set of core entities and interactions.

  • Spatial entities: waypoints, clues, search sectors, last-seen points, extraction paths, and route geometry.
  • Team entities: teammate status, unit counts, task assignments, local time, and operational confidence.
  • Information entities: witness statements, transcripts, key facts, feed detections, and confidence scores.
  • System entities: GPS quality, comms state, battery level, live-feed availability, and alert readiness.

Current posture

Early development, but with more grounded concept direction.

These pages still describe an early platform, not a fielded product. The difference now is that the visual story is more aligned to the intended workflows, which makes future technical discussion and user feedback more useful.

Next

Later
