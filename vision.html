The vision

Mission awareness that moves with the search.

The updated concept set makes the A² vision more concrete: responders should be able to navigate the terrain, capture witness knowledge, correlate remote sensor cues, and align on a shared plan without breaking focus on the mission.

[ NAVIGATE · CAPTURE · CORRELATE · COORDINATE ]

The north star

Turn fragmented SAR information into one shared field-intelligence loop.

A² is not trying to replace radios, maps, or existing tools. The vision is to connect them more effectively so the responder in motion and the team behind them can act on a clearer shared picture.

01

Guide movement

Show where the team is, where clues were found, what sector is active, and where extraction or rendezvous points sit.

02

Structure information

Capture interview details in usable form so witness statements can become mission-relevant facts instead of isolated notes.

03

Fuse evidence

Compare detections and cues across video feeds, teammates, and AI assistance to raise confidence and reduce noise.

04

Align the team

Keep route plans, status, and tasking synchronized so the whole search effort works from the same map and intent.

Workflow 01

Navigate the terrain with mission context in view.

The navigation concept shows more than a compass. It combines waypoints, team positions, clue markers, a search sector, extraction direction, GPS and comms status, and a mini topo map so the responder can move with a better sense of the overall search picture.

  • Search sector and last-seen context tied to place.
  • Waypoint, teammate, clue, and extraction distance cues.
  • Persistent awareness of GPS, comms, battery, and team status.

Workflow 02

Capture witness information as structured mission intelligence.

The interview-assist concept introduces a critical part of the search workflow: a responder talking to a witness while the system records what was said, extracts key facts, compares it to other team interviews, and shows how those statements fit the operational map.

  • Live transcript capture for the field interviewer.
  • Key facts like time, clothing, direction, and last-seen location.
  • Cross-reference and consensus scoring across witness reports.

Workflow 03

Correlate live feeds and elevate the right alert.

The multi-feed concept shows how A² could connect what teams are already using: teammate XR feeds, drones, trail cameras, canine-mounted cameras, and AI detection overlays. The goal is not more video for its own sake, but faster confirmation and clearer team alerts.

  • Multiple live sources available from one wearable view.
  • Cross-feed correlation and confidence scoring for possible subject cues.
  • Broadcast-ready alert workflow for high-priority findings.

Workflow 04

Bring the team together around a shared 3D operational map.

The collaborative-map concept shifts from the individual wearer to the team. Responders can look at the same terrain model, inspect route plans, see team statuses, and sync changes back out to the field. That creates a more tangible common operating picture than voice-only coordination.

  • Shared 3D terrain, clues, last-seen markers, drone and K9 context.
  • Route plan summaries and synchronized task distribution.
  • A team-level view that complements the individual XR wearer.

Development principles

Built for field reality, not for a demo alone.

These concepts are still aspirational, but they are intended to be operationally grounded. The goal is to mature them with responder feedback, practical hardware decisions, and honest evaluation of where XR and AI actually help.

01

Responder-first

Keep interfaces glanceable and mission-relevant rather than cognitively heavy.

02

Spatial by design

Represent information as places, paths, sectors, cues, and relationships in the environment.

03

AI as support

Use AI to summarize, correlate, and prioritize, not to replace responder judgment.

04

Path to pilots

Move from concept clarity toward prototype refinement and future field collaboration.

Next step
