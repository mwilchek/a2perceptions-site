The vision

Responder awareness should move with the mission.

A² Perceptions is exploring how wearable XR, shared spatial context, and field command views can help responders understand where to go, what others have seen, and what has changed.

[ A² FIELD INTELLIGENCE ]

The problem

Critical mission context is often trapped outside the responder’s field of view.

During search, rescue, police, EMS, and disaster operations, the people moving through the environment often depend on radios, handheld maps, command posts, and separate sensor feeds. Those tools are important, but they can create friction when the mission is dynamic, spatial, and time-sensitive.

Teams need more than location.

Knowing where a responder is does not always show what they found, what they cleared, or what command needs them to do next.

Information moves through bottlenecks.

Radio calls, screenshots, and verbal updates can slow down when multiple teams need the same context at the same time.

Field scenes change quickly.

Routes, hazards, clues, resources, and priorities can shift faster than static maps or separate screens can capture.

The opportunity

XR can become a mission layer, not a distraction.

The goal is not to replace radios, maps, or command systems. The goal is to make the most useful mission context visible at the right time and place, while keeping responders focused on the environment and people in front of them.

Development principles

Built for field reality.

A² is intentionally taking an early, workflow-driven approach. The platform should meet responders where they already operate and mature through field conversations before becoming a pilot-ready product.

01

Responder-first

Keep attention on the mission, not on manipulating a device.

02

Spatial by design

Represent mission information as places, routes, zones, and shared markers.

03

Compatible path

Build around commercially available hardware and practical field workflows.

04

Honest maturity

Develop the system carefully, validate assumptions, and avoid overclaiming readiness.
